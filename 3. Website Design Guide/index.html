Color Schemes

Monochromatic

Each color only varies in saturation.

Used for designs where contrast is not necessary such as charts and graphs.

Analogous

Consists of thee adjacent colors (usually 30 hue apart).

Great for creating softer palletes with less contrast. Useful for creating warm or cool schemes.

Complementary

Two colors that are on opposite sides.

Used to create the highest possible amount of contrast.

Three equidistant colors.

Another option to create a high level of contrast. Best practice to choose a primary color.

Maintains a high level of contrast while allowing for more nuance.
